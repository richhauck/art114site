Responsive Event Website (200 points)

Project Description:

Using Adobe XD, you will design a responsive design for a mock event websites. Your designs should include two interactive piecesâ€”one for desktop (up to 1920px width) and one for mobile (320px width to represent portrait mode on mobile devices).

The finished HTML page should display responsively, include responsive image techniques, and include an SVG logo. You will use CSS3 media queries to target mobile devices, tablets, and desktop computers.
Development Requirements:

  1. Your design should work responsively with no horizontal scrolling.
  2. The SVG version of the logo must be used.
  3. Your design should use at least one (1) non-system web font.
  4. Images should scale responsively.
  5. Your page should include a favicon and a touch icon.
  6. Your design should have a functional responsive navigation.
  7. Your finished project should be published on your website.

Process:

  1. Research and Sitemap Find 3-5 event websites and write a critique of them on your WordPress site. Establish a site map of a minimum of 5 pages.
  2. Sketches/Wireframing. You will develop a desktop and mobile wireframes outlining all of the major elements in your site.
  3. Design. Upon wireframe approval, you will create designs based on your wireframes. You should be designing the desktop and mobile versions in parallel, and ensure that both present identical messages.
  4. Design Critique. You will take part in an in-class design critique where you will provide constructive criticism to your classmates on their designs, as well as receive feedback on yours.
  5. Revisions. You will revise the design, if necessary, to address improvements uncovered during the critique.
  6. Development. You will proceed to develop the website in your local environment. Upon completion, the finished site should be published on your website.
  7. Final Critique. You will participate in a final group critique where we review each of the sites.
